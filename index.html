AutoGPS

AutoGPS: Automated Geometry Problem Solving via
Multimodal Formalization and Deductive Reasoning



Introduction

Geometric problem solving presents distinctive challenges in artificial intelligence, requiring not only robust multimodal comprehension but also exceptional mathematical reasoning capabilities. Current methodologies exhibit deficiencies in processing multimodal information while being constrained by limited interpretability and reliability in their reasoning processes.

To address these challenges, we propose AutoGPS, a neuro-symbolic framework that synergistically integrates the multimodal understanding strengths of neural methods with the rigorous reasoning capabilities of symbolic systems for automated geometric reasoning. Specifically, AutoGPS employs a multimodal formalization framework to systematically translate geometry problems described through textual and visual modalities into structured formal language representations. This is followed by deductive symbolic reasoning operations that execute mathematically verifiable derivations, yielding human-readable solving procedures.

Extensive experimental evaluations demonstrate that our method establishes new state-of-the-art performance on benchmark datasets (Geometry3K and PGPS9K), while significantly enhancing interpretability through verifiable proof generation. Furthermore, leveraging enhanced interpretability and deductive rigor, we identified and rectified inherent inconsistencies within the Geometry3K dataset.

Circle O has a radius of 13 inches. Radius O B is perpendicular to chord C D which is 24 inches long. Find O X. 

Equals(LengthOf(Line(C, D)), 24)
Equals(LengthOf(Line(O, B)), 13)
Equals(RadiusOf(Circle(O)), 13)
IsChordOf(Line(C, D), Circle(O))
Perpendicular(Line(B, X), Line(D, X))
Perpendicular(Line(O, B), Line(C, D))
PointLiesOnCircle(B, Circle(O, radius_0_0))
PointLiesOnCircle(C, Circle(O, radius_0_0))
PointLiesOnCircle(D, Circle(O, radius_0_0))
PointLiesOnLine(X, Line(B, O))
PointLiesOnLine(X, Line(C, D))
Find(LengthOf(Line(O, X)))
                    
1: Known facts: start ⇒ 13 = radius_0_0, CD, DO, D on ⊙(O, radius_0_0), CX ⊥ OX, CO, OX, ∠OCX, C on ⊙(O, radius_0_0), ∠OCD, 24 = CD, CX, X on CD
2: Point On Circle Def: D on ⊙(O, radius_0_0) ⇒ radius_0_0 = DO
3: Definition of Triangle: DO, CD, CO ⇒ △CDO
4: Point On Circle Def: C on ⊙(O, radius_0_0) ⇒ radius_0_0 = CO
5: Definition of Triangle: CO, OX, CX ⇒ △COX
6: Same Angle: ∠OCD, X on CD ⇒ ∠OCD = ∠OCX
7: Transtivity of Equivalence: radius_0_0 = DO, 13 = radius_0_0 ⇒ 13 = DO
8: Law of Cosines: △CDO ⇒ (CD ** 2 + CO ** 2) - DO ** 2 = 2 * CD * CO * cos(∠OCD)
9: Transtivity of Equivalence: 13 = radius_0_0, radius_0_0 = CO ⇒ 13 = CO
10: Sin Function Definition: ∠OCX, △COX, CX ⊥ OX ⇒ OX / CO = sin(∠OCX)
11: Substitution: 13 = CO, ∠OCD = ∠OCX, (CD ** 2 + CO ** 2) - DO ** 2 = 2 * CD * CO * cos(∠OCD), 13 = DO, 24 = CD ⇒ (13 ** 2 + 24 ** 2) - 13 ** 2 = 13 * 2 * 24 * cos(∠OCX)
12: Solve Univariate Equation: (13 ** 2 + 24 ** 2) - 13 ** 2 = 13 * 2 * 24 * cos(∠OCX) ⇒ 0.39 = ∠OCX
13: Substitution: 0.39 = ∠OCX, 13 = CO, OX / CO = sin(∠OCX) ⇒ sin(0.39) = OX / 13
14: Solve Linear Equation System: sin(0.39) = OX / 13 ⇒ 5 = OX
Final answer: 5

Tangent(Line(C, E), Circle(C, radius_0_0))
Find(x)
                    
1: Known facts: start ⇒ B on ⊙(C, radius_0_0), Tangent(AF, ⊙(C, radius_0_0)), B on AC, AC, AF, 8 = AF, CF, x = AB, 6 = BC, F on ⊙(C, radius_0_0)
2: Point On Circle Def: B on ⊙(C, radius_0_0) ⇒ radius_0_0 = BC
3: Line Segment Split: B on AC ⇒ AC = AB + BC
4: Definition of Triangle: AF, CF, AC ⇒ △ACF
5: Tangent Definition: Tangent(AF, ⊙(C, radius_0_0)), F on ⊙(C, radius_0_0) ⇒ AF ⊥ CF
6: Point On Circle Def: F on ⊙(C, radius_0_0) ⇒ radius_0_0 = CF
7: Transtivity of Equivalence: radius_0_0 = BC, 6 = BC ⇒ 6 = radius_0_0
8: Substitution: x = AB, AC = AB + BC, 6 = BC ⇒ 6 + x = AC
9: Pythagorean Theorem: AF ⊥ CF, △ACF ⇒ AC ** 2 = AF ** 2 + CF ** 2
10: Transtivity of Equivalence: 6 = radius_0_0, radius_0_0 = CF ⇒ 6 = CF
11: Substitution: 6 + x = AC, 6 = CF, AC ** 2 = AF ** 2 + CF ** 2, 8 = AF ⇒ 6 ** 2 + 8 ** 2 = (6 + x) ** 2
12: Solve Univariate Equation: 6 ** 2 + 8 ** 2 = (6 + x) ** 2 ⇒ 4 = x
Final answer: 4

PointLiesOnLine(R, Line(J, L))

Equals(LengthOf(Line(M, J)), 3a)
Equals(LengthOf(Line(M, L)), 45)
Equals(LengthOf(Line(J, K)), 2b+3)

Equals(MeasureOf(Angle(R, L, K)), 70)
Parallelogram(J,K,L,M)
Equals(LengthOf(Line(J,K)),2b+3)
Equals(LengthOf(Line(J,M)),3a)
Find(MeasureOf(Angle(K,J,L)))
                    
1: Known facts: start ⇒ Parallelogram(J, K, L, M), ∠JLM, pi * (30 / 180) = ∠RLM, ∠LJK, R on JL
2: Parallelogram Opposite Lines Parallel: Parallelogram(J, K, L, M) ⇒ JK || ML
3: Same Angle: R on JL, ∠LJK ⇒ ∠LJK = ∠RJK
4: Same Angle: ∠JLM, R on JL ⇒ ∠JLM = ∠RLM
5: Alternate Interior Angle Theorem: JK || ML ⇒ ∠JLM = ∠LJK
6: Transtivity of Equivalence: ∠LJK = ∠RJK, ∠JLM = ∠LJK ⇒ ∠JLM = ∠RJK
7: Transtivity of Equivalence: ∠JLM = ∠RLM, pi * (30 / 180) = ∠RLM, ∠JLM = ∠RJK ⇒ pi * (30 / 180) = ∠RJK
8: Transtivity of Equivalence: pi * (30 / 180) = ∠RJK, ∠LJK = ∠RJK ⇒ pi * (30 / 180) = ∠LJK
9: Evaluate: pi * (30 / 180) = ∠LJK ⇒ 0.52 = ∠LJK
Final answer: 0.52

Problem Description

Find B D if B F bisects ∠ A B C and A C || E D, B A = 6, B C = 7.5, A C = 9, and D E = 9. 

PointLiesOnLine(C, Line(B, D))
PointLiesOnLine(F, Line(A, C))
PointLiesOnLine(F, Line(E, B))
Parallel(Line(E, D), Line(A, C))

BisectsAngle(Line(B,F),Angle(A,B,C))

Equals(LengthOf(Line(B,A)),6)
Equals(LengthOf(Line(B,C)),7.5)
Equals(LengthOf(Line(A,C)),9)
Equals(LengthOf(Line(D,E)),9)
Find(LengthOf(Line(B,D)))
                    
1: Known facts: start ⇒ 7.5 = BC, 9 = DE, F on BE, ∠DBF, F on AC, C on BD, ∠BCA, AB, ∠CBF = ∠FBA, AC, BC, ∠DBE, 9 = AC, 6 = AB, AC || ED
2: Line Segment Split: F on AC ⇒ AC = AF + CF
3: Same Angle: ∠DBF, C on BD ⇒ ∠CBF = ∠DBF
4: Same Angle: ∠BCA, F on AC ⇒ ∠BCA = ∠BCF
5: Definition of Triangle: AB, BC, AC ⇒ △ABC
6: Same Angle: F on BE, ∠DBE ⇒ ∠DBE = ∠DBF
7: Corresponding Angle Theorem: AC || ED ⇒ ∠BCA = ∠BDE, ∠BED = ∠BFC
8: Transtivity of Equivalence: 9 = AC, AC = AF + CF ⇒ 9 = AF + CF
9: Triangle Angle Bisector Theorem: ∠CBF = ∠FBA, △ABC ⇒ BC / AB = CF / AF
10: Transtivity of Equivalence: ∠DBE = ∠DBF, ∠CBF = ∠DBF ⇒ ∠CBF = ∠DBE
11: Transtivity of Equivalence: ∠BCA = ∠BDE, ∠BCA = ∠BCF ⇒ ∠BCF = ∠BDE
12: Substitution: BC / AB = CF / AF, 6 = AB, 7.5 = BC ⇒ 7.5 / 6 = CF / AF
13: Angle-Angle Similarity Theorem: ∠CBF = ∠DBE, ∠BCF = ∠BDE, ∠BED = ∠BFC ⇒ △BCF ~ △BDE
14: Similar Definition: △BCF ~ △BDE ⇒ ∠BED = ∠BFC, ∠CBF = ∠DBE, sim_ratio_triangle_b_c_f_triangle_b_d_e = CF / DE, sim_ratio_triangle_b_c_f_triangle_b_d_e = BC / BD
15: Substitution: sim_ratio_triangle_b_c_f_triangle_b_d_e = CF / DE, 9 = DE ⇒ sim_ratio_triangle_b_c_f_triangle_b_d_e = CF / 9
16: Substitution: sim_ratio_triangle_b_c_f_triangle_b_d_e = BC / BD, 7.5 = BC ⇒ sim_ratio_triangle_b_c_f_triangle_b_d_e = 7.5 / BD
17: Solve Linear Equation System: 7.5 / 6 = CF / AF, sim_ratio_triangle_b_c_f_triangle_b_d_e = CF / 9, 9 = AF + CF ⇒ 0.56 = sim_ratio_triangle_b_c_f_triangle_b_d_e
18: Transtivity of Equivalence: 0.56 = sim_ratio_triangle_b_c_f_triangle_b_d_e, sim_ratio_triangle_b_c_f_triangle_b_d_e = 7.5 / BD ⇒ 0.56 = 7.5 / BD
19: Solve Linear Equation System: 0.56 = 7.5 / BD ⇒ 13.5 = BD

Problem Description

Find Q P. 

PointLiesOnLine(Q, Line(M, P))
PointLiesOnLine(N, Line(M, O))
Parallel(Line(Q, N), Line(P, O))
Equals(LengthOf(Line(M, N)), 6)
Equals(LengthOf(Line(M, Q)), 5)
Equals(LengthOf(Line(O, N)), 3+\frac{3}{5})
Equals(LengthOf(Line(P, Q)), x)
Find(LengthOf(Line(Q,P)))
                    
1: Known facts: start ⇒ 3 + 3 / 5 = NO, 5 = MQ, ∠QMO, ∠OPM, 6 = MN, ∠PMO, ∠OPM, NQ || OP, N on MO, ∠NQM, 3.14 = pi, x = PQ, Q on MP
2: Reverse Angle Definition: ∠OPM ⇒ ∠OPM = 2 * pi - ∠MPO
3: Corresponding Angle Theorem: NQ || OP ⇒ ∠MNQ = ∠MOP, ∠MPO = ∠MQN
4: Line Segment Split: N on MO ⇒ MO = MN + NO
5: Same Angle: N on MO, ∠QMO ⇒ ∠QMN = ∠QMO
6: Reverse Angle Definition: ∠NQM ⇒ ∠NQM = 2 * pi - ∠MQN
7: Same Angle: Q on MP, ∠OPM ⇒ ∠OPM = ∠OPQ
8: Same Angle: Q on MP, ∠PMO ⇒ ∠PMO = ∠QMO
9: Line Segment Split: Q on MP ⇒ MP = MQ + PQ
10: Substitution: 3 + 3 / 5 = NO, MO = MN + NO, 6 = MN ⇒ 6 + (3 + 3 / 5) = MO
11: Substitution: ∠NQM = 2 * pi - ∠MQN, 3.14 = pi ⇒ ∠NQM = 2 * 3.14 - ∠MQN
12: Substitution: 3.14 = pi, ∠MPO = ∠MQN, ∠OPM = 2 * pi - ∠MPO, ∠OPM = ∠OPQ ⇒ ∠OPQ = 2 * 3.14 - ∠MQN
13: Transtivity of Equivalence: ∠QMN = ∠QMO, ∠PMO = ∠QMO ⇒ ∠PMO = ∠QMN
14: Substitution: 5 = MQ, MP = MQ + PQ, x = PQ ⇒ 5 + x = MP
15: Solve Linear Equation System: 6 + (3 + 3 / 5) = MO ⇒ 9.6 = MO
16: Transtivity of Equivalence: ∠NQM = 2 * 3.14 - ∠MQN, ∠OPQ = 2 * 3.14 - ∠MQN ⇒ ∠NQM = ∠OPQ
17: Transtivity of Equivalence: ∠NQM = ∠OPQ, ∠OPM = ∠OPQ ⇒ ∠NQM = ∠OPM
18: Angle-Angle Similarity Theorem: ∠NQM = ∠OPM, ∠MNQ = ∠MOP, ∠PMO = ∠QMN ⇒ △MNQ ~ △MOP
19: Similar Definition: △MNQ ~ △MOP ⇒ sim_ratio_triangle_m_n_q_triangle_m_o_p = MQ / MP, ∠PMO = ∠QMN, sim_ratio_triangle_m_n_q_triangle_m_o_p = MN / MO, ∠MNQ = ∠MOP, ∠MPO = ∠MQN
20: Substitution: sim_ratio_triangle_m_n_q_triangle_m_o_p = MQ / MP, 5 = MQ, 5 + x = MP ⇒ sim_ratio_triangle_m_n_q_triangle_m_o_p = 5 / (5 + x)
21: Substitution: sim_ratio_triangle_m_n_q_triangle_m_o_p = MN / MO, 9.6 = MO, 6 = MN ⇒ 6 / 9.6 = sim_ratio_triangle_m_n_q_triangle_m_o_p
22: Transtivity of Equivalence: sim_ratio_triangle_m_n_q_triangle_m_o_p = 5 / (5 + x), 6 / 9.6 = sim_ratio_triangle_m_n_q_triangle_m_o_p ⇒ 6 / 9.6 = 5 / (5 + x)
23: Solve Linear Equation System: 6 / 9.6 = 5 / (5 + x), x = PQ ⇒ 3 = PQ
Final answer: 3
